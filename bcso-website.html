Who We Are

About the Department

The Blaine County Sheriff's Office is a serious, semi-realistic law enforcement roleplay community built on professionalism, teamwork, and immersive gameplay.

We operate within one of San Andreas' most active FiveM servers, providing structured law enforcement roleplay across the entirety of Blaine County — from Sandy Shores to Paleto Bay. Our deputies go through a proper academy pipeline before hitting the streets.

We partner with other emergency services agencies including the LSFD, EMS, and civilian factions to create a living, breathing world of roleplay for our entire community.

Whether you're a veteran roleplayer or brand new to FiveM, BCSO has a place for you — as long as you bring dedication and respect for the craft.

Professionalism
We hold ourselves to the same standards as real-world law enforcement roleplay. Proper radio etiquette, callsigns, and chain of command are core to who we are.
Immersive Roleplay
From high-speed pursuits to complex investigative scenarios, our department runs detailed, immersive operations across the county.
Community First
BCSO is built around a welcoming, drama-free community. We mentor new members, celebrate milestones, and grow together.

Standards & Conduct

Rules & SOP

All BCSO members are expected to uphold these standards at all times. Violations may result in demotion or removal.

01
General Conduct
  • Treat all players — civilian or LEO — with respect.
  • No OOC harassment, discrimination, or hate speech.
  • Always remain in character while on duty.
  • Do not abuse admin powers or department tools.
02
Patrol Standards
  • Wear department-approved uniform at all times.
  • Use proper 10-codes and radio discipline.
  • Cadets and Deputy I must partner with a senior deputy.
  • Lights & sirens only when legally justified.
03
Use of Force
  • Force must be proportional to the threat level.
  • Lethal force is last resort — exhaust all alternatives.
  • All officer-involved shootings require a report.
  • No RDM (random deathmatch) under any circumstance.
04
Activity Requirements
  • Minimum 4 hours of patrol per week.
  • LOA required for any absence over 7 days.
  • Attend mandatory monthly department meetings.
  • Training exercises participation is expected.
05
Chain of Command
  • All orders from superior ranks must be followed on duty.
  • Disputes go through proper HR channels — not public.
  • Do not go above your direct supervisor without reason.
  • Promotions and discipline are handled by command only.
06
Vehicles & Equipment
  • Only use department-approved vehicles while on duty.
  • No reckless driving unless in active pursuit.
  • Report vehicle damage via the department form.
  • Personal vehicles prohibited during official patrol.
